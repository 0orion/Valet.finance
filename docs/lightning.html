Step 3: Open a Lightning Channel

To use the Lightning Network, you need to open a channel. This is a one-time setup that allows you to send and receive Bitcoin instantly with minimal fees.

What is a Lightning Channel?

A Lightning channel is like a payment channel that allows you to make instant Bitcoin transactions. Think of it as opening a tab at a bar - you can make multiple transactions without settling each one individually.

How to Open a Channel

  1. From the home screen, tap on the "Lightning" tab at the bottom of the screen.
  2. Tap on "Open Channel" or the "+" button.
  3. Choose a node to connect to. You can use the default node or select a different one.
  4. Enter the amount of Bitcoin you want to commit to the channel (minimum 0.001 BTC).
  5. Review the channel opening fee and confirm the transaction.
Tip: Start with a small amount (0.001-0.005 BTC) for your first channel. You can always open more channels later.

Channel States

  • Pending: The channel is being opened. This can take a few minutes to a few hours.
  • Active: The channel is ready to use for sending and receiving Bitcoin.
  • Closing: The channel is being closed. This can take a few hours to complete.

Channel Management

You can manage your channels from the Lightning tab:

  • View channel status and balance
  • Close channels when needed
  • Monitor channel health and capacity
Important: Keep some Bitcoin in your on-chain wallet for channel opening fees and emergency closures.
